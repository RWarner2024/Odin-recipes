Sisig

Sisig is a juicy, fatty, and extremely flavorful dish that originates from the Philippines. It is traditionally served on a cast iron plates that makes the tender pork belly sizzle as you eat. One can top it with many things but it is best with chopped chili, calamansi, green onion and a fresh cracked egg on top. Enjoy with a steaming bowl of white rice.

Ingredients

    18oz pork belly 1 tbsp white vinegar salt and pepper(to taste) 2 tbsp oil 1 large onion(diced) 3 garlic cloves(finely chopped) 2 tbsp calamansi juice(to taste) 1 tbsp Maggi(to taste) 2 tsp soy sauce 3 tbsp mayonnaise(optional) green and red chili(chopped) 1 egg green onion(opional, chopped)

Steps

    Slice pork belly into 3 inch thick strips. Rub vinegar all over and season with salt and Pepper. Cook in the oven or air fryer at 200 degrees C/400 degrees F for 30 minutes until crispy on the outside. You can also pan-fry the pork in hot il until crispy. Let it rest and cool, then chop into small pieces. Heat oil in a skillet until hot and toss in onions and garlic and saute until translucent. Add chili and cook until fregrant. Add pork to the pan and stir fry for 2 - 3 minutes. Add soy sauce, Maggi, half calamansi juice and pepper. Stir until meat is well coated in sauce. Turn off heat and add mayonnaise if using and coat meat. Top with egg and let residual heat cook it. Top with green onion and serve with rice.
